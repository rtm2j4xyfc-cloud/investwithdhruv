Why Building Credit Matters

Your credit score is used by landlords, lenders, phone providers, and even some employers. In Canada, scores range from 300 to 900 — above 660 is considered good. Newcomers and students start with no score at all. The fastest fix: get a credit card, use it for small purchases, and pay the full balance every single month.

The Story

Like a lot of Canadians, I didn't grow up with a strong foundation in personal finance. Nobody explained TFSAs to me in school. Nobody walked me through what an ETF actually is.

Every time I searched for guidance, I found resources written for Americans — different accounts, different tax rules. The Canadian experience was almost invisible online.

So I did the work myself. I spent years reading and researching until I had a clear picture of what actually works for a regular Canadian investor. And then I thought — why should everyone have to figure this out alone?

Invest With Dhruv is the resource I wish existed when I started — Canada-specific, clearly written, and designed so you can walk into any financial decision with genuine confidence.
